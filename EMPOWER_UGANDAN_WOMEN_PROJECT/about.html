Our Mission

Our mission is to provide rural women in Uganda with the skills, tools, and opportunities they need to build sustainable livelihoods through agriculture and entrepreneurship. By equipping women with these resources, we aim to close the gender gap and support economic growth.

Our Vision

To create a world where every woman in Uganda has access to resources and opportunities that empower her to achieve financial stability and take leadership roles in her community.

Objectives of the organization

  • Empower Women Farmers: Provide tools, resources, and training to increase women's productivity and economic independence in agriculture.
  • Promote Equal Access: Advocate for equal access to land, finance, technology, and markets for women in agriculture.
  • Support Sustainable Farming: Encourage environmentally friendly farming practices through education and community initiatives.
  • Foster Leadership: Develop leadership opportunities for women in rural areas and agricultural communities.
  • Advocate for Policy Change: Push for policies that support gender equality, land rights, and financial inclusion for women in agriculture.
  • Build Networks: Create a supportive network for women in agriculture to connect, collaborate, and share knowledge.
  • Enhance Food Security: Strengthen food security by improving the productivity and sustainability of women-led farming.

Our services

  • Training Programs : Online courses on sustainable farming, financial literacy, and agricultural technology.

  • Incentives : Access to grants, loans, and educational materials for women in agriculture.

  • Platform: Connect with mentors, agribusinesses and support groups.

  • Marketplace : A space for women farmers to sell their produce and products .

  • Advocacy Campaigns : Raise awareness about gender equality and promote supporting policies.

  • Consultation Services : Expert advice on farming techniques, business planning, and land rights .

  • Opportunities : Listings of employment and leadership roles in agriculture .
